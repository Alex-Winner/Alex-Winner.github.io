Stereo motion capture

×

Stereo motion capture, often abbreviated as "stereo mocap," is a cutting-edge technology used to capture three-dimensional motion and depth information using two synchronized web cameras. This technique has gained popularity in various fields, including animation, gaming, virtual reality, and biomechanics, due to its affordability and versatility. Stereo mocap leverages the principle of stereoscopy, which mimics the human visual system by capturing two slightly offset images of the same scene. In the context of motion capture, this means using two web cameras positioned at a fixed distance from each other to record the movement of objects or individuals in 3D space.
